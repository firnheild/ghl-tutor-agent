# Calendar and Booking

Skill level: Level 3

Learning objective: Create booking flows with availability, appointment reminders, and no-show reduction.

## Plain-English Explanation

Calendars let leads book time without back-and-forth messages. A good booking system makes the next step obvious, confirms the appointment, reminds the lead, and gives staff enough context.

## Key Terms

- Calendar: The booking system that controls available days, times, appointment length, and who receives the appointment.
- Appointment: A scheduled meeting, consultation, service visit, or call.
- Availability: The times when a business or staff member is open for booking.
- Buffer: Extra time before or after appointments so staff are not overloaded.
- Booking link: A link a lead uses to choose an available time.
- Appointment reminder: A message sent before the appointment so the lead does not forget.
- No-show: When a lead books but does not attend.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Choose the right calendar type for the business need.
2. Set availability, buffers, appointment length, and team assignment rules.
3. Create a booking link and connect it to the right form or funnel.
4. Add confirmation and reminder messages.
5. Test the full flow as if you are a lead.

## Example Client Situation

A grooming shop books 60-minute appointments with a 15-minute cleanup buffer. The owner wants SMS reminders 24 hours and 2 hours before the visit, plus an internal notification for first-time customers.

## Common Mistakes

- Forgetting buffers and causing back-to-back overload.
- Sending reminders without appointment details.
- Not testing timezone and availability rules.
- Letting leads book services the business does not offer.

## Practice Task

Write the reminder schedule for a dental consultation booked three days from now. Include timing and message purpose.

## Quick Quiz

Question: Why should you test a booking link as a lead?

Expected answer: To confirm availability, reminders, contact creation, notifications, and appointment details all work before the client uses it.

## Hire-Ready Skill Note

Appointment systems are easy to sell and useful for local businesses. A beginner who can reduce no-shows with clean reminders is already solving a real business problem.
