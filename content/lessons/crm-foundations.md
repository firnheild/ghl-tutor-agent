# CRM Foundations

Skill level: Level 1

Learning objective: Organize contacts with tags, smart lists, custom fields, notes, and tasks.

## Plain-English Explanation

The CRM is the contact database. Every lead, customer, and prospect should have enough information for the team to know who they are, what they need, and what should happen next. Clean CRM setup prevents messy automation.

## Key Terms

- Contact: The person record inside the CRM. It usually stores name, phone, email, tags, notes, tasks, and custom fields.
- Lead: A contact who might buy or book but has not fully become a customer yet.
- Tag: A label added to a contact. Tags help organize contacts and can trigger automation. Examples: New Lead, Dental Implant Interest, VIP, No Show.
- Custom field: A specific data field created for the business. Examples: Pet Type, Preferred Service, Budget, Property Address, Insurance Provider.
- Smart list: A saved filtered list of contacts. Examples: New Leads This Week, Missing Phone Number, Whitening Leads, No Appointment Booked.
- Note: A human-written detail about the contact, such as "Prefers afternoon calls" or "Asked about pricing."
- Task: A reminder or assignment for a team member to do something, such as call, review, clean up, or follow up.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Create or import contacts with name, phone, email, and source.
2. Add tags only when they help segmentation or automation.
3. Use custom fields for information that changes by business, such as preferred service or budget.
4. Use smart lists to filter useful groups, such as new leads, no appointment booked, or VIP customers.
5. Add notes for human context and tasks for follow-up responsibility.

## Example Client Situation

A dental clinic imports 800 old leads. You create smart lists for leads with phone numbers, leads missing email addresses, and leads interested in whitening. You add a cleanup task before any reactivation campaign.

## Common Mistakes

- Using tags as random labels instead of automation signals.
- Putting structured data in notes instead of custom fields.
- Importing contacts without checking duplicates.
- Creating smart lists with unclear filters.

## Practice Task

For a grooming shop, suggest three tags, two custom fields, and one smart list that would help staff follow up.

## Quick Quiz

Question: When should you use a custom field instead of a tag?

Expected answer: Use a custom field when you need to store a specific value, such as pet type, service interest, budget, or appointment preference.

## Hire-Ready Skill Note

CRM cleanup is a real paid task. If you can organize contact records and explain why each field or tag exists, you can help agencies and local businesses immediately.
