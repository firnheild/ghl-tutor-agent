# Forms, Funnels, and Lead Capture

Skill level: Level 4

Learning objective: Capture leads through forms, surveys, landing pages, and thank-you pages.

## Plain-English Explanation

Forms and funnels collect lead information. The landing page creates interest, the form captures details, and the thank-you page tells the lead what happens next. A good lead capture system also sends the data into the CRM cleanly.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Define the offer or reason the lead should submit details.
2. Keep the landing page focused on one action.
3. Ask only for fields the business truly needs.
4. Route the submission into the right tags, custom fields, pipeline stage, and follow-up workflow.
5. Use the thank-you page to confirm the next step.

## Example Client Situation

A dental clinic runs a free implant consultation offer. The funnel has a headline, short benefits, a form with name, phone, email, preferred day, and a thank-you page that invites booking.

## Common Mistakes

- Asking too many form questions.
- Sending every form into the same generic workflow.
- Forgetting the thank-you page.
- Writing landing page copy without a clear offer.

## Practice Task

Create the form fields and thank-you page message for a real estate buyer guide lead magnet.

## Quick Quiz

Question: What are the three basic parts of a lead capture funnel?

Expected answer: A focused landing page, a form or survey to collect details, and a thank-you page that explains the next step.

## Hire-Ready Skill Note

Funnel builders who understand CRM routing are more useful than designers who only make pages look nice. The goal is captured leads and reliable follow-up.
