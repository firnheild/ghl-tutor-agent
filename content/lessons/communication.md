# Communication

Skill level: Level 5

Learning objective: Write practical SMS and email follow-up messages, understand the Conversations inbox, and know when automation should hand a lead back to a human.

## Plain-English Explanation

Communication is where leads become real conversations. A lead may submit a form, book an appointment, reply to a text, ask about price, or say they are not ready yet. GoHighLevel helps the business keep those messages in one place so staff can respond without losing context.

A beginner does not need to write fancy copy. The goal is to write clear messages that sound human, ask for one next action, and stop when the lead replies or books.

Good communication work protects the client from two common problems: slow follow-up and awkward over-messaging.

## Key Terms

- Conversations inbox: The shared message area where SMS, email, chat, calls, and replies can be reviewed.
- SMS: A short text message sent to a lead or customer.
- Email template: A reusable email message for confirmations, follow-up, reminders, or nurture.
- Follow-up: A message sent after a lead takes an action or fails to take the next step.
- Reply handling: The rule for what happens after a lead responds.
- Lead nurture: A planned series of helpful messages that keeps a lead warm over time.
- Opt-out: A lead's request to stop receiving messages.
- Handoff: The moment automation stops and a staff member should take over manually.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Identify why the lead is being contacted: new inquiry, missed call, no booking, appointment reminder, old lead, or post-service review.
2. Write the first message in simple language.
3. Ask for one next action, such as "Would you like the morning or afternoon slot?"
4. Decide what should happen if the lead replies.
5. Decide what should happen if the lead does not reply.
6. Keep SMS short and use email for longer details.
7. Add an internal notification or task when staff need to respond.
8. Test messages with a fake contact before using real leads.

## Example Client Situation

A dental clinic gets new implant consultation leads from Facebook ads. The lead submits a form but does not book yet. A useful communication plan sends a fast SMS, waits a short time, sends an email with consultation details, then notifies staff if the lead replies or asks about pricing.

## Common Mistakes

- Sending messages that sound robotic or too aggressive.
- Asking three questions in one SMS.
- Continuing nurture messages after a lead replies.
- Forgetting to notify staff when a lead asks a real question.
- Writing long SMS messages that should be emails.
- Sending messages without checking consent, opt-out behavior, and local business rules.

## Beginner-Friendly Formula

Use this structure when you feel stuck:

1. Acknowledge the reason for the message.
2. State the next helpful step.
3. Ask one simple question.

Example: "Hi Maria, thanks for asking about dental implants. The next step is a short consultation so the clinic can check your case. Do you prefer a morning or afternoon appointment?"

## Practice Task

For a grooming shop, write a follow-up sequence for a new lead who submitted a grooming form but has not booked. Include one SMS, one email fallback, one reply handling rule, and one staff handoff note.

## Easy Practice Version

If you are a complete beginner, write only three lines:

1. The first SMS.
2. What the staff should do if the lead replies.
3. When the automation should stop.

## Hirable Completion Standard

You meet the hire-ready expectation for this level when you can:

- Write a clear first SMS that sounds human.
- Explain what the Conversations inbox is used for.
- Decide when a workflow should stop after a reply.
- Draft an email fallback that supports the SMS.
- Add a staff handoff rule for real questions.
- Test the message using fake lead data.

## Quick Quiz

Question: Why should a follow-up automation detect replies?

Expected answer: It should detect replies so the lead can leave the automated nurture path and receive human help instead of getting duplicate or awkward messages.

## Hire-Ready Skill Note

Many clients do not need advanced automation first. They need faster, clearer, more reliable follow-up. If you can write simple messages and prevent awkward automation behavior, you can already help appointment-based businesses.
