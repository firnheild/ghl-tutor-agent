# Pipeline and Sales Process

Skill level: Level 2

Learning objective: Map sales stages into pipelines and understand opportunities.

## Plain-English Explanation

A pipeline shows where each lead is in the sales process. An opportunity is the deal record inside that pipeline. Good stages are based on real actions, not vague feelings.

## Key Terms

- Pipeline: A visual sales tracker with stages. It shows where leads are in the process from new inquiry to won or lost.
- Opportunity: The deal record connected to a contact. One contact can exist in the CRM, while the opportunity tracks the sales process.
- Stage: One step in the pipeline. Examples: New Lead, Contacted, Booked, Proposal Sent, Won, Lost.
- Lead status: A simple label showing the contact's general status, such as new, active, won, lost, or unqualified.
- Manual movement: A person moves the opportunity to another stage after checking what actually happened.
- Automated movement: A workflow moves the opportunity when a clear event happens, such as form submitted or appointment booked.

## Step-by-Step Instructions

1. List the client sales steps from new inquiry to won or lost.
2. Turn each meaningful step into a pipeline stage.
3. Decide when a person should move the opportunity manually.
4. Decide when automation can move the opportunity safely.
5. Add lost reasons so reporting is useful.

## Example Client Situation

A real estate agent needs stages for New Buyer Lead, Contacted, Consultation Booked, Actively Searching, Offer Made, Closed Won, and Closed Lost. A form can create the opportunity, but the agent should manually move serious deal stages.

## Common Mistakes

- Creating too many stages that no one updates.
- Moving opportunities automatically when human judgment is needed.
- Confusing contact status with opportunity stage.
- Not defining what each stage means.

## Practice Task

Design a five-stage pipeline for a dental implant consultation offer. Keep stage names practical.

## Quick Quiz

Question: What is the difference between a contact and an opportunity?

Expected answer: A contact is the person record. An opportunity is the sales or deal record that tracks where that person is in a pipeline.

## Hire-Ready Skill Note

Pipeline mapping is valuable because clients want visibility. If you can turn messy sales conversations into clear stages, you become more than a button-clicker.
